Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 5, 2026 · Last Updated: May 5, 2026

AMPD Technologies LLC ("AMPD," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of every person who uses our platform — especially the student-athletes we serve. This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and — just as importantly — what we never do. It applies to the AMPD mobile application, the AMPD web portal at app.liveampd.com, our marketing website at liveampd.com, and all related services (collectively, the "Service").

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and agree to this Privacy Policy. If you are under 18, please review this policy with your parent or guardian.

1. Our Commitment to Privacy

AMPD is a readiness and development platform for high school athletic programs. We are not a healthcare provider, a medical record system, or a student information system.

We design our platform around the principle of data minimization: we collect the smallest amount of information needed to support athlete development, and we never collect data that is not strictly necessary for the Service. Specifically, AMPD does not collect, store, or persist any of the following:

  • Academic grades, GPA, transcripts, or class schedules
  • Athletic training records, injury history, return-to-play status, or treatment notes
  • Concussion or other medical incident records
  • Mental-health diagnoses, counseling notes, or therapy records
  • Disciplinary records, IEPs, or 504 plans
  • Social Security numbers, home addresses, or financial account information of student-athletes
  • Precise geolocation of any user

For schools that subscribe to our optional Academics product, academic information is fetched live from the school's Student Information System ("SIS") on demand, displayed to authorized users, and discarded — it is never written to AMPD's database. Only a single "eligible / ineligible" status flag is retained on the user record.

We comply with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), and the Illinois Student Online Personal Protection Act (SOPPA) where applicable. We do not sell, rent, or share personal information for advertising or marketing purposes.

2. Information We Collect

A. Information Provided by You or Your Organization

When you or your athletic program create an account or use the Service, we may collect:

  • Identity: first name, last name, email address, role within the program (athlete, parent, coach, athletic director, IT administrator)
  • Organization context: school or program name, team and group assignments, sport, coaching role title
  • Self-reported readiness scores: daily check-in responses across five categories — Energy, Stress, Soreness, Focus, Confidence — each on a 1–5 scale, plus an optional 1–5 mood indicator
  • Program content: messages, announcements, polls, workout logs, calendar events, mindset content, and meeting requests created or sent through the Service
  • Profile information: profile photo (optional)
  • Legal consent records: timestamps and IP address when accepting our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
  • Contact form submissions: name, email, phone number, school name, role, and message when you submit a demo request through our website

B. Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we automatically collect:

  • Device information: device type, operating system, app version
  • Log data: IP address, access timestamps, requested URLs (for security and abuse prevention)
  • Push notification tokens: only if you opt in to receive notifications
  • Session cookies: a single first-party session cookie (ampd.sid) on the .liveampd.com domain to keep you signed in

We do not use third-party advertising trackers, behavioral targeting, or cross-site tracking technologies. We do not track precise device location.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect strictly to:

  • Provide, operate, and improve the Service
  • Display readiness data and program content to authorized users based on their role (see Section 5)
  • Generate aggregated, non-identifying insights for coaches and program administrators
  • Send Service-related communications (account onboarding, push notifications, password resets, contract renewals)
  • Respond to support requests and demo inquiries
  • Monitor for, investigate, and prevent abuse, fraud, and security incidents
  • Comply with legal and contractual obligations

We do not use personal information for third-party advertising. We do not sell your data. Ever. We do not use student data to train, fine-tune, or improve any artificial-intelligence model.

4. How We Protect Your Information

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect your personal information.

  • Encryption in transit: all traffic to and from the Service is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encryption at rest: our primary database (MongoDB Atlas) encrypts all data at rest using AES-256. SIS API credentials are additionally field-level encrypted using AES-256-CBC with a separate key before being stored
  • US-only data residency: all production data is hosted in the United States. Backups are encrypted and remain in U.S.-based regions only
  • Authentication: session-based authentication with HTTP-only, secure cookies. Passwords are hashed using bcrypt before storage. Single sign-on via ClassLink uses OAuth 2.0 with PKCE
  • Role-based access controls: all data access is enforced at the server level and scoped by organization. One school cannot access another school's data; one role cannot see data outside its scope
  • Automatic data minimization: sensitive transient data such as daily check-ins, messages, and workout completions automatically expire after 90 days. Push notification logs expire after 30 days
  • Background checks and access controls for AMPD personnel: employee access to production data is limited to those with a legitimate operational need and is logged
  • Vulnerability management: dependencies are scanned for known vulnerabilities; critical patches are applied promptly

Breach Notification

While we work hard to protect your information, no system is 100% secure. If we become aware of a confirmed security breach affecting personal information of student-athletes, parents, or staff at your school, we will:

  • Notify the affected school or district's Data Privacy Officer (or named contact) within 30 days of confirming the breach, in accordance with Illinois SOPPA
  • Include in that notification: the nature of the breach, the categories of data affected, the approximate number of individuals impacted, the remediation steps we have taken, and recommended steps for affected users
  • Cooperate fully with the school or district in any further notifications they elect to make to affected individuals or regulators

5. Data Visibility and Access Controls

AMPD is built around athlete development. Every piece of data we collect exists to help coaches and programs better support their athletes — and access is strictly limited to that purpose.

We use a role-based access system to ensure that each user only sees the information relevant to their role within the program. Athletic Directors see organization-level information; head coaches see their teams; assistant coaches and staff see only what their head coach has explicitly granted; athletes see their own data and the program content shared with them; parents see a summary of their linked athletes only.

Data access is enforced at the server level, not just the user interface. Your program's data stays within your program.

6. AMPD Support Access

From time to time, AMPD platform staff may need to access your school's account to help with onboarding, troubleshoot a technical issue, or assist with a support request. We are transparent about how this works.

  • Limited to the web portal. AMPD staff support access is limited to the web portal at app.liveampd.com. AMPD staff cannot access mobile-app sessions on athlete or parent devices under any circumstances. Mobile-app issues are investigated via consented screen-share with the affected user, never by impersonation.
  • Per-organization opt-out. Your Athletic Director controls a toggle in the AD Settings panel that enables or disables AMPD support access at any time. When disabled, AMPD staff are blocked from starting a support session against any user in your school, with no override available outside the AD's control.
  • Comprehensive vendor audit log. Every support session — including the start, end, and every write action taken while logged in — is recorded in a separate vendor audit log maintained by AMPD. Each entry captures the staff member's identity, the user account being assisted, the IP address, the timestamp, and the specific action taken.
  • Not visible in your school's activity log. Support actions taken while AMPD staff are logged in as a user do not appear in your school's activity log. They are captured in the vendor audit log only, so the school's activity log reflects actions taken by your own people.
  • On-demand transparency. A complete report of all AMPD support sessions on your school, including every action taken, is available on request. Email privacy@liveampd.com.
  • Persistent visual indicator. Whenever an AMPD staff member is logged in as a user, a persistent banner is displayed at the top of every page identifying the impersonator and the impersonated user.
  • Athletes and parents are not impersonated. The AMPD platform admin's "log in as" function is technically limited to roles that exist in the web portal: Athletic Director, head coach, staff, and IT administrator.

7. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

AMPD may be used by student-athletes under the age of 13. We are committed to complying with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act and take the following steps:

  • Accounts for minors are created through their athletic program, not directly by the child
  • We collect only the minimum information necessary to provide the Service
  • We do not require children to disclose more information than is reasonably necessary
  • We do not use personal information from children for advertising or marketing
  • Parents may request to review, modify, or delete their child's personal information by contacting us at privacy@liveampd.com or by contacting their school directly

If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 13 without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

8. FERPA Compliance

When AMPD is used by a school or school district, we may receive student information that is protected under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act. In these cases:

  • AMPD acts as a "school official" with a legitimate educational interest, as defined under FERPA
  • We use student data solely to provide the Service to the school or district
  • We do not use student data for any purpose other than the educational purpose for which it was provided
  • We do not share student data with third parties except as necessary to provide the Service (see Section 10)
  • Schools and districts retain control over their student data and may request its deletion at any time
  • Parents have the right to inspect and review their child's education records held by AMPD on behalf of the school. Requests should be directed to the school first; we will support the school in fulfilling such requests

9. Illinois SOPPA Compliance

For schools and districts in the State of Illinois, AMPD complies with the Illinois Student Online Personal Protection Act (SOPPA). Specifically, we:

  • Provide written notice of any contracted school's right to inspect, review, and request deletion of student data
  • Provide breach notification to the affected school or district within 30 days of confirming a breach affecting student data (see Section 4)
  • Do not engage in targeted advertising to students based on data covered by SOPPA
  • Do not sell or rent student data, ever
  • Do not use student data to amass a profile of a student for purposes outside the educational context
  • Make available, on request, a complete list of subprocessors with access to student data and the role each one plays (see Section 10)
  • Maintain a publicly accessible Privacy Policy (this document) describing our data handling practices, and notify schools at least 30 days before any material changes take effect

A signed Illinois Student Data Privacy Agreement (IL-NDPA) and Exhibit E ("General Offer of Privacy Terms") are available for adoption by Illinois districts on request from privacy@liveampd.com.

10. Subprocessors and Data Sharing

We do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone.

We use a small number of carefully selected third-party service providers ("subprocessors") to operate the Service. Each subprocessor is contractually obligated to protect your information, has access only to the minimum data needed to perform its function, and may not use that data for any other purpose.

Other Disclosures

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, such as to comply with a subpoena, court order, or other legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of AMPD, our users, or others. We will provide affected schools with notice of any such legal request to the extent permitted by law.

In the event that AMPD is acquired, merged, or sells substantially all of its assets, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify schools and districts via email at least 30 days before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

11. Artificial Intelligence

AMPD uses Anthropic's Claude API to power optional features such as AI-generated workout plans and mindset session content. We have built our integration with strict data-minimization principles:

  • No athlete names, email addresses, or other identifiers are sent to the AI service
  • No daily check-in scores, free-text athlete responses, or other student-specific information is sent
  • AI prompts contain only generic context such as the type of training, sport, focus area, and tone, plus coach-provided generic prompts
  • AMPD has confirmed with Anthropic that prompts and responses are not used to train AI models

For more information on Anthropic's data practices, visit anthropic.com/privacy.

12. Data Retention and Deletion

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to provide the Service.

  • Active accounts: data is retained for the duration of your school's contract
  • Daily check-ins: automatically expire 90 days after submission
  • Messages, workouts, mindset uploads, announcements: automatically expire 90 days after creation
  • Push notification logs: automatically expire 30 days after creation
  • Archived athletes (graduated, transferred, or removed): retained for 365 days for restoration, then automatically purged with full cascade across every related record
  • Inactive schools: if a school discontinues the Service, we retain account data for up to 12 months to allow for reactivation, after which all data is deleted
  • End of contract: upon contract termination, the school may export all of its data, after which AMPD will permanently delete the data within 30 days and provide a certificate of destruction on request
  • Deletion requests: schools, districts, parents, or individual users may request deletion of their data at any time by emailing support@liveampd.com. We respond to all deletion requests within 30 days

When data is deleted, it is permanently removed from our active systems. Residual copies in encrypted backups are overwritten through normal backup rotation within 30 days.

13. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information
  • Delete your personal information
  • Object to or restrict certain processing of your data
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on your consent
  • Opt out of non-essential communications
  • Receive a complete report of AMPD support sessions on your school's account (see Section 6)

If your personal data has been provided to us by your school or athletic organization, please contact your organization first. They will direct the request to AMPD if needed, and we will support them in fulfilling it. To exercise any rights directly with AMPD, email privacy@liveampd.com. We respond to all requests within 30 days.

14. Cookies and Tracking

The AMPD web portal and mobile app use a single first-party session cookie (ampd.sid) on the .liveampd.com domain to keep you signed in. We do not use third-party advertising trackers, behavioral targeting cookies, cross-site tracking technologies, or third-party analytics on the AMPD platform. You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings.

15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will provide notice via email and through the Service at least 30 days before the changes take effect. Your continued use of the Service after the updated policy becomes effective constitutes your acceptance of the changes.

The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy is always current. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
