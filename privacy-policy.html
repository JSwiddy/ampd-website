Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 12, 2026

AMPD Technologies LLC ("AMPD," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of every person who uses our platform — especially the student-athletes we serve. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use the AMPD mobile application, website, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and agree to this Privacy Policy. If you are under 18, please review this policy with your parent or guardian.

1. Our Commitment to Privacy

AMPD is a readiness and development platform for athletic programs. We are not a healthcare provider, and we do not collect medical records, diagnoses, or health information. The data we collect consists of self-reported readiness scores and program-related information used to support athlete development.

We take the privacy of minors seriously. AMPD respects and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) where applicable. We do not sell, rent, or share personal information with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes.

2. Information We Collect

A. Information Provided by You or Your Organization

When you or your athletic program create an account or use the Service, we may collect:

  • Account information: Name, email address, and role (e.g., athlete, coach, athletic director, parent)
  • Organization information: School or program name, team and group assignments
  • Self-reported readiness scores: Daily check-in responses across five categories (Energy, Stress, Soreness, Focus, Confidence) on a 1–5 scale
  • Content: Messages, announcements, workout logs, and calendar events created through the Service
  • Profile information: Profile photo (optional)
  • Contact form submissions: Name, email, phone number, school name, role, and message when you submit a demo request through our website

B. Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we may automatically collect:

  • Device information: Device type, operating system, and app version
  • Usage data: Feature usage, session frequency, and interaction patterns
  • Log data: IP address, browser type, and access timestamps
  • Push notification tokens: If you opt in to receive notifications

We use this information to maintain, improve, and secure the Service. We do not track the precise geolocation of any user.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, operate, and improve the Service
  • Display readiness data and trends to authorized users based on their role (see Section 5)
  • Generate aggregated, non-identifying insights for coaches and program administrators
  • Send Service-related communications (e.g., onboarding, notifications, account updates)
  • Respond to support requests and demo inquiries
  • Ensure the safety and security of the platform
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not use personal information for third-party advertising. We do not sell your data. Ever.

4. How We Protect Your Information

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect your personal information, including:

  • Encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS)
  • Secure, session-based authentication
  • Role-based access controls that restrict data visibility by user type
  • All data scoped by organization — one program cannot access another program's data
  • Regular review of data collection, storage, and processing practices

While we work hard to protect your information, no system is 100% secure. If we become aware of a security breach affecting your personal data, we will notify affected users and organizations as required by law.

5. Data Visibility and Access Controls

AMPD is built around athlete development. Every piece of data we collect exists to help coaches and programs better support their athletes.

We use a role-based access system to ensure that each user only sees the information relevant to their role within the program. Athletes, coaches, administrators, and parents each have different levels of visibility — and no user can access data from a team or organization they are not a part of.

Data access is enforced at the server level, not just the interface. Your program's data stays within your program.

6. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

AMPD may be used by student-athletes under the age of 13. We are committed to complying with COPPA and take the following steps to protect children's privacy:

  • Accounts for minors are created through their athletic program, not directly by the child
  • We collect only the minimum information necessary to provide the Service
  • We do not require children to disclose more information than is reasonably necessary
  • We do not use personal information from children for advertising or marketing
  • Parents may request to review, modify, or delete their child's personal information by contacting us

If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 13 without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

7. FERPA Compliance

When AMPD is used by a school or school district, we may receive student information that is protected under FERPA. In these cases:

  • AMPD acts as a "school official" with a legitimate educational interest, as defined under FERPA
  • We use student data solely to provide the Service to the school or district
  • We do not use student data for any purpose other than the educational purpose for which it was provided
  • We do not share student data with third parties except as necessary to provide the Service (see Section 8)
  • Schools and districts retain control over their student data and may request its deletion at any time

8. Who We Share Information With

We do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone.

We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

Service Providers

We use third-party service providers for essential functions such as hosting, data storage, email delivery, and image hosting. These providers only have access to the minimum information necessary to perform their function and are contractually obligated to protect it. They do not have the right to use your personal information beyond what is necessary to assist us in providing the Service.

Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, such as to comply with a subpoena, court order, or other legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of AMPD, our users, or others.

Business Transfers

In the event that AMPD is acquired, merged, or sells substantially all of its assets, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you via email or prominent notice on the Service before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

9. Artificial Intelligence

AMPD uses third-party artificial intelligence services to power certain features. Specifically, coaches use AI to generate workout plans and training content, and AI is used to generate mindset session content delivered to athletes. These features do not send any athlete data, biometric data, or personally identifiable information to any AI service. AI-generated content is created using only coach-provided inputs such as training type and focus area. This functionality is powered by Anthropic's Claude AI. For more information on Anthropic's data practices, visit anthropic.com/privacy.

10. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service to your organization. Specifically:

  • Active accounts: Data is retained for the duration of the organization's use of the Service
  • Inactive accounts: If an organization discontinues the Service, we will retain data for up to 12 months to allow for reactivation, after which it will be deleted
  • Deletion requests: Schools, districts, parents, or individual users may request deletion of their data at any time by contacting us

When data is deleted, it is permanently removed from our active systems. Residual copies in encrypted backups are overwritten through normal backup rotation.

11. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information
  • Delete your personal information
  • Object to or restrict certain processing of your data
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on your consent
  • Opt out of non-essential communications

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information in Section 14 below. We will respond to your request within 30 days.

If your personal data has been provided to us by your school or athletic organization, please contact your organization directly to exercise your rights. We will support them in fulfilling your request.

12. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Maintain your session and keep you logged in
  • Understand how visitors use our website (via Microsoft Clarity)
  • Improve performance and user experience

We do not use cookies for third-party advertising or behavioral targeting. You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will provide notice through the Service or by email at least 30 days before the changes take effect. Your continued use of the Service after the updated policy becomes effective constitutes your acceptance of the changes.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

14. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, want to exercise your rights, or need to report a concern, please contact us:

AMPD Technologies LLC

Website: liveampd.com/schedule-demo

We will respond to all inquiries within 30 days.
