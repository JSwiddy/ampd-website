How We Protect It

Three pillars of platform security.

Real names, real moderation. United States hosting, encrypted at every layer. Role-based access enforced on the server.

01

Real names. Real moderation.

AMPD has no anonymous accounts. Every athlete, coach, and staff member is invited by their school and signs in as themselves.

Knowing every poster is a known member of the school community removes most of the conditions that make platforms unsafe for students. Coaches and athletic directors can remove any post or message in real time, and every administrative action is recorded in an audit log.

02

US-only. Encrypted. Isolated.

Application served by Vercel (US), database on MongoDB Atlas (US). All replication and backup remains inside the United States.

TLS 1.2+ on all client-server traffic. AES-256 at rest, with personally identifying fields additionally encrypted at the application layer with a separate key. SIS API credentials are field-level encrypted. Every piece of student data is scoped by organization, and cross-organization access is impossible by design.

03

Role-based access, server-enforced.

Readiness is a coaching tool by design. Athletes complete a daily check-in, but they do not see readiness analytics, trend graphs, or aggregated scores for themselves. Coaches see their team. Athletic directors see the program. Parents see compliance status and schedule, never raw scores.

Every endpoint is gated by role and organization on the server, not in the client. Athletic trainers and counselors are activated only when the school enables them, with scoped permissions defined by the head coach.

Compliance & Standards

Built to the standards your district answers to.

AMPD operates under the legal framework that governs any vendor handling student records in the United States.

FERPAFamily Educational Rights & Privacy Act

AMPD operates as a school official with legitimate educational interest under district direction. Student records are accessed and processed only as needed to deliver the service the school has authorized.

COPPAChildren's Online Privacy Protection Act

Accounts are created via the school, never directly by minors. The school-consent model applies. AMPD does not collect personal information from children outside of what the district has authorized for educational purposes.

Illinois SOPPAStudent Online Personal Protection Act

30-day breach notification SLA, parent inspection process, no targeted advertising, no profiling for non-educational purposes, no sale of student data. SDPC IL-NDPA v1.0a available for counter-signature on request.

Common Questions

Answers to what districts and parents ask most.

Does AMPD track location or device activity?

No. The mobile app does not request or collect device location, browsing history, contacts, photo library, microphone, or calendar access. The only permissions requested are push notifications and optional Face ID or fingerprint for faster sign-in. Biometric data never leaves the device.

Who can see my child's check-in data?

Readiness analytics are coach-only by design. Athletes complete the daily check-in but do not see scores, trends, or analytics for themselves. Coaches see aggregated readiness for their team. Parents see compliance and schedule, never raw scores. Athletic directors see the program. Athletic trainers and counselors only see what the head coach has explicitly enabled. Cross-organization access is impossible by design.

Are messages and posts moderated?

Yes. Every account is real-named, no anonymous posting. Any user can report inappropriate content directly inside the app. Coaches and athletic directors can remove any post or message in real time, and every administrative action is recorded in an audit log available to the district.

Where is the data hosted, and is it encrypted?

All AMPD data is hosted in the United States on MongoDB Atlas, with the application served by Vercel (US). Replication and backup remain inside the United States. Data is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher and at rest using AES-256. Personally identifying fields (names, contact information, emergency contacts, parent-submitted form responses, and signatures) are additionally encrypted at the application layer with a separate key. SIS API credentials are likewise field-level encrypted.

What happens to data when a school stops using AMPD?

Within 30 days of a written request, AMPD permanently deletes all student data from primary databases and backups. A Certificate of Destruction is provided to the district as confirmation.

Does AMPD advertise to students or sell student data?

No, and never. AMPD does not run advertisements. Student data is never used to target or direct advertising in any form. AMPD does not sell, lease, or otherwise transfer student data to third parties for marketing or commercial purposes.

How does AMPD handle a security breach?

Within 30 days of a confirmed breach, AMPD notifies the district's named contact in writing per Illinois SOPPA. Notification includes the nature of the breach, the data involved, the steps being taken, and recommended actions for the district.
